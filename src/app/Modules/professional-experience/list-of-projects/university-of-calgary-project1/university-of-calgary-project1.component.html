An electrical substation presents a high risk of electrocution to animals (e.g. birds and reptiles) that can damage electrical components. To reduce this risk, the best solution is to insulate all connections, in particular those located on top of insulators, with precisely fitted covers. In any substation, each insulator can have a different size (radius) and form (number of rings), so each cover must be custom designed. In order to design these covers, this study focuses on the automatic detection of insulators and the extraction of their position and morphological parameters using laser scanner data.

To detect the insulators , four steps are required: data collection and registration of the point cloud acquired from multiple stations; data cleaning; cylinder detection and insulator detection. A terrestrial laser scanner "Leica HDS 6100" was used to collect data from an electrical substation located in Airdrie, Alberta Canada. The substation had to be scanned from seven different locations.
